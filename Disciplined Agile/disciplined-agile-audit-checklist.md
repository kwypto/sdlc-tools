# Disciplined Agile (DA) SDLC Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to audit whether a team’s Disciplined Agile way of working is context-appropriate, governed, and outcome-driven.

## 1. Way-of-Working Selection and Fit
- Confirm the team explicitly selected a DA lifecycle (Agile, Lean, Continuous Delivery, Exploratory, Program, etc.) based on context.
- Verify lifecycle rationale is documented against business goals, risk profile, regulatory demands, and team constraints.
- Assess whether the chosen way of working is revisited as context changes (scale, criticality, technology, organization).
- Check that process goals and decision points are transparent to both delivery and governance stakeholders.
- Ensure the selected lifecycle is applied consistently rather than a nominal label over ad hoc execution.

## 2. DA Process Goal Coverage
- Verify essential DA process goals are addressed (explore scope, coordinate activities, produce consumable solution, and govern delivery).
- Confirm guidance exists for architecture, security, testing, deployment, data, and support-related decisions.
- Assess whether goal trade-offs are made explicitly with rationale and accountable decision owners.
- Check that process blades are applied where needed (enterprise architecture, portfolio management, operations, finance, compliance).
- Ensure there are no unmanaged blind spots where critical process goals are repeatedly skipped.

## 3. Contextual Tailoring and Guardrails
- Confirm team practices are tailored for domain criticality, compliance burden, team distribution, and product complexity.
- Verify tailoring decisions include explicit constraints/guardrails to prevent quality or control erosion.
- Assess whether tailoring is evidence-based (metrics, incidents, delivery outcomes) rather than preference-driven.
- Check that role expectations, workflows, and policies align with the tailored approach.
- Ensure governance stakeholders accept the tailored model and can audit it without ambiguity.

## 4. Inception and Foundation Readiness
- Verify inception outcomes include shared vision, stakeholder alignment, funding assumptions, and success criteria.
- Confirm initial architecture strategy, risk profile, and dependency landscape are identified and documented.
- Check readiness of delivery foundations: tooling, environments, team capability, and initial backlog/work item flow.
- Assess whether compliance, security, and operational needs are designed in from inception.
- Ensure unresolved foundational risks are tracked with owners and mitigation plans before scaling execution.

## 5. Execution Flow and Coordination
- Confirm work is visualized and managed with flow-aware policies (WIP limits, pull strategy, explicit class-of-service where relevant).
- Verify cross-team and external dependencies are actively coordinated with clear owners and commitments.
- Assess cycle efficiency by reviewing wait states, handoff delays, rework loops, and blocked work aging.
- Check integration frequency and feedback loop speed across development, testing, and operations.
- Ensure planning horizons (near-term and medium-term) are balanced to support both responsiveness and predictability.

## 6. Enterprise Awareness and Alignment
- Verify alignment with enterprise priorities, portfolio governance, architecture principles, and operational standards.
- Confirm effective interfaces with shared services (security, legal, procurement, data governance, SRE/ops).
- Assess whether local team optimization conflicts with enterprise-level goals or constraints.
- Check escalation paths for enterprise conflicts and whether resolution outcomes are documented.
- Ensure strategic changes (policy, architecture, platform) are reflected promptly in team ways of working.

## 7. Built-in Quality and Technical Excellence
- Confirm quality is embedded throughout flow: automated checks, peer review rigor, and shift-left testing.
- Verify non-functional requirements are continuously validated, not deferred to late-stage hardening.
- Assess security practices (threat modeling, dependency governance, vulnerability remediation, secure coding) in normal cadence.
- Check management of technical debt with explicit backlog visibility, prioritization, and remediation cadence.
- Ensure quality gate failures trigger corrective action rather than informal acceptance of risk.

## 8. Governance, Compliance, and Evidence
- Verify governance is right-sized: lightweight enough for flow, strong enough for risk and regulatory obligations.
- Confirm key decisions, approvals, and exceptions are recorded with rationale and accountable owners.
- Check segregation of duties and control points across build, test, approval, and deployment activities.
- Assess evidence generation and retention practices for audits (traceability, test evidence, approval logs, change history).
- Ensure compliance findings are tracked to closure with verified corrective/preventive actions.

## 9. Learning Culture and Continuous Evolution
- Confirm teams run structured improvement experiments with hypotheses, metrics, and review cycles.
- Verify communities of practice, coaching, and knowledge-sharing mechanisms are active and outcome-oriented.
- Assess whether retrospectives and improvement forums address systemic constraints, not only local issues.
- Check that improvement work is prioritized and capacity-allocated instead of deferred behind feature pressure.
- Ensure the team periodically reassesses and updates its DA way of working based on measurable delivery and quality outcomes.
