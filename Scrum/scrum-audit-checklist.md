# Scrum SDLC Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to audit Scrum process health, delivery quality, and continuous improvement effectiveness.

## 1. Roles and Ceremony Discipline
- Confirm Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Development Team responsibilities are explicitly defined and understood.
- Verify Scrum events occur at the intended cadence: Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, and Sprint Retrospective.
- Assess whether ceremonies are outcome-focused (clear decisions and actions) rather than status-only meetings.
- Ensure attendance and participation are consistent, with role-appropriate contributions.
- Check that event outcomes are documented (sprint goals, action items, decisions, risks) and traceable.

## 2. Product Backlog Quality and Readiness
- Verify the product backlog is continuously prioritized based on value, risk, and strategic objectives.
- Confirm user stories are well-formed, testable, and include clear acceptance criteria.
- Check backlog refinement discipline: sizing, dependency discovery, risk identification, and split strategy.
- Ensure non-functional requirements and technical enablers are represented (not deferred indefinitely).
- Assess whether backlog items entering sprint planning meet agreed Definition of Ready.

## 3. Sprint Planning and Execution Control
- Confirm each sprint has a clear sprint goal tied to business outcomes.
- Verify sprint commitment is capacity-informed, realistic, and based on team availability.
- Check active management of blockers, dependencies, and scope changes during the sprint.
- Assess work-in-progress discipline and whether multitasking is constrained to maintain flow.
- Ensure progress visibility through maintained boards, burndown/burnup, and transparent impediment tracking.

## 4. Definition of Ready (DoR) and Definition of Done (DoD)
- Verify DoR and DoD are explicit, shared, and reviewed periodically as team maturity evolves.
- Confirm DoD includes code quality, testing, security checks, documentation updates, and integration readiness.
- Check that no story is accepted as done if DoD conditions are partially met or deferred informally.
- Assess consistent DoR/DoD enforcement across all team members and sprint cycles.
- Ensure exceptions to DoR/DoD are documented, justified, and approved by the right stakeholders.

## 5. Engineering and Quality Practices
- Confirm source control, branching, peer review, and merge policies support quality and traceability.
- Verify CI/CD pipelines run consistently with actionable build/test feedback and low flakiness.
- Assess automated testing depth (unit, integration, end-to-end) and alignment with risk-critical areas.
- Check static analysis and security checks are integrated into normal workflow, not only pre-release.
- Review defect prevention patterns: coding standards, pair/mobing where used, and technical debt controls.

## 6. Increment Validation and Stakeholder Feedback
- Verify each sprint produces a potentially releasable increment that meets acceptance criteria.
- Confirm Sprint Review demonstrates working software rather than status slides or design-only artifacts.
- Check stakeholder feedback capture, triage, and incorporation into backlog prioritization.
- Ensure acceptance/rejection decisions are explicit and recorded for audited traceability.
- Assess whether recurring feedback themes drive measurable product or process changes.

## 7. Metrics, Predictability, and Flow Performance
- Verify the team tracks meaningful metrics (velocity trend, cycle time, lead time, throughput, escaped defects).
- Assess whether metrics are used for forecasting and improvement, not for punitive individual comparisons.
- Check variance patterns (spillover, carryover, unplanned work) and root-cause actions.
- Ensure release predictability is reviewed against historical data and confidence ranges.
- Validate service-level expectations (incident response, defect turnaround) are monitored and met.

## 8. Release and Operational Governance
- Confirm release criteria are explicit and include quality, security, compliance, and business readiness checks.
- Verify release planning includes rollback strategy, communication plan, and support readiness.
- Check change approvals (where required) are timely and evidence-backed, without bypassing controls.
- Ensure production monitoring and alerting are active at release time with clear ownership.
- Validate post-release verification and hypercare activities are executed and documented.

## 9. Retrospective Effectiveness and Continuous Improvement
- Confirm retrospectives produce specific, prioritized, and owner-assigned improvement actions.
- Verify retro actions are tracked across sprints and closed with visible evidence of completion.
- Assess whether process experiments are run with clear hypotheses and measurable success criteria.
- Check improvement topics include both delivery flow and engineering quality, not only team dynamics.
- Ensure the team periodically revisits working agreements, DoD/DoR, and practices based on observed outcomes.
